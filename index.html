ABOUT

CONTEXT

Pulled into systems, then into building.

I've always been the kind of person who gets pulled into too many things at once. Chess, coding, finance, psychology, games, drawing. At some point the pattern stopped being "interested in a lot of stuff" and started being "wants to understand how systems work," and that turned into building.

I'm studying FinTech at FAST NUCES right now, not because I had a clean career plan but because finance and software felt like the two fields where I could build things that do something real. I've spent the last year making a full-stack finance app, messing around with ML, and figuring out what I don't know yet, which is a lot.

I also spent three years teaching Python and chess to over 150 students, which is probably the reason I can explain things without making people's eyes glaze over. And I'm a national-level chess player, 2200+ rated. That shaped how I think more than anything else: staying calm when things get complicated, reading a few moves ahead, and learning from losses without getting emotional about it.
